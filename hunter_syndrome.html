Description


Also known as MDS 11, Hunter Syndrome is a genetic disorder that targets males. Their body is unable to break down sugars that build up in the bones, skin, and other body parts. This build up can lead to brain damage and immobility of the bodily functions.

Images


Effects & Symptoms


The disease will affect physical features, making the boy look more crippled or rounded. Thick bones, curled digits speech impediments, and digestive system issues will plague the boy for the rest of his life.

Treatment & Cure


There is no cure, only certain treatment plans to make the pain not a severe. These treatments include: ERT, bone marrow or umbilical cord transplants, and physical therapists of multiple kinds. This disease is very serious and can be fatal for many untreated men.
